Plain answers

Keep it clear from day one.

The site should feel easy to enter, clear about what happens next, and honest about what happens only after clinical assessment.

Is there a heavy upfront fee?

No. The signup page is intentionally low resistance. Any consult fee or service cost should be shown clearly before a patient books or commits.

Do patients see products before signing up?

No. The public site stays simple. Product information belongs inside the future patient portal and must still sit behind proper clinical assessment and lawful pharmacy dispensing.

Is Crossroads tied to brand kickbacks?

The site is written around a non-biased model: patients first, doctor matching second, products only where clinically appropriate.

What CRM should this use?

Start with a secure lead pipeline and minimal public-form data. The database should be connected only when the storage, access control, and privacy workflow are ready.
